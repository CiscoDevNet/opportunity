Census Accelerate

Census Accelerate pairs creative agencies and content strategists with community-based organizations to generate innovative digital content and campaigns for the 2020 Census.

Background

The Decennial Census is the largest peacetime mobilization in the United States and one of the most consequential pillars of U.S. democracy. It determines congressional redistricting and apportionment, the distribution of $675B of annual federal funding, and the formation of countless decisions for policy, business, and planning. The stakes are high — the effects of the 2020 Census will be felt for at least a decade. In a rapidly changing world, in the face of new and increasing challenges and opportunities, our mandate remains as it has been since 1790: to ensure that everyone is counted once, only once, and in the right place.

But there are serious challenges to ensuring a complete and accurate count. They include:

  • Rapidly declining response rates, especially within populations considered “Hard to count” such as children under 5, urban and rural low-income households, households with poor internet access, and more
  • Decreased trust in the government
  • Increased social polarization
  • A lack of up-to-date, accurate, and quality content on the internet, and an increasing volume of disinformation
  • The rising cost of attention

What is Accelerate?

Potential Solutions

We invite a wide range of creative digital solutions to generate positive awareness and encourage specific hard-to-reach populations to be counted. A detailed brief will be provided prior to the sprint kick-off.

Participants

creative agencies

Traditional agency project teams, in-house marketing teams, coalition of boutique agencies, and university students.

user advocates

Community-based and national advocacy organizations; individuals representing target audiences

subject matter experts

Experts in Census, certain Hard-to-Count populations, or another relevant area of expertise

The strategy and planning will be developed by creative teams. We encourage a broad variety of groups to participate, such as:

  • A project team from a traditional agency working a fixed number of hours
  • An in-house marketing team looking to flex its creative muscles in a new way
  • A coalition of boutique agencies focusing on their local region
  • University students from a design or media program building a portfolio

Community-based organizations that serve hard-to-count populations will play the role of user advocates to ensure that content serves people in an appropriate cultural, historical, and language context.

We are also assembling experts and foundations to participate in framing and distributing the message.

Target Audiences

A sample of Accelerate's target groups includes:

  • Families with children under five
  • Rural and tribal populations
  • Millennials

Benefits


For creative teams:

  • Deliver on CSR and mission-related goals by strengthening civic engagement and directly increasing Census participation among of hard-to-count populations.
  • Strengthen professional capacity by broadening team members’ skills and exposing their practice to new audiences.
  • Improve the accuracy of the Census, and learning more about the data that form a foundation of so many agencies’ regular research.
  • Work alongside other purpose-driven creative teams.
  • Have your work featured at open press Demo Day with other industry and government leaders.

For user advocates:

  • Receive customized, high-quality strategy and materials at no cost to support your complete count efforts.
  • Learn new marketing and messaging concepts by working intensively with top-flight strategic and creative talent.
  • Educate influential professionals on the unique challenges and opportunities of the population you serve.
  • Work alongside other purpose-driven creative teams.
  • Visibility for your organization at Demo Day with open press.

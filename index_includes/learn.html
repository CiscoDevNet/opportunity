The Open Opportunity Data development process seeks to expose the current, unequal geography of opportunity – the fact that today, opportunity is not evenly distributed. We are committed to changing that, so that a young person's zip code does not determine her destiny.

Now, as a nation, we don't promise equal outcomes, but we were founded on the idea everybody should have an equal opportunity to succeed. No matter who you are, what you look like, where you come from, you can make it. That's an essential promise of America. Where you start should not determine where you end up.

—Barack Obama

What is "Opportunity?"

For the purposes of Open Opportunity Data development, opportunity is defined as:

  • access to the resources and services that a family, individual, or community needs to thrive, and
  • freedom from the stressors that detract from the family, individual, or community's ability to thrive.

What's been happening?

This community is the result of a White House effort to further opportunity mobility in neightborhoods.

In early 2016, various federal and local government agencies collaborated with community advocate organizations, subject matter experts, and a number of private sector companies to create online tools that can be used to investigate and illustrate the state of opportunity an the neighborhood level.

This effort culminated with a convening at the White House on March 7. Learn more! fact sheet, video, photos (neighborland?)
